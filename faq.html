Frequently Asked Questions

1. What types of coffee do you offer?

We offer a diverse range of coffee types, including:

  • Espresso: A concentrated coffee brewed by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee beans.
  • Americano: Espresso mixed with hot water for a milder flavor.
  • Latte: Espresso combined with steamed milk and a small amount of milk foam.
  • Cappuccino: Similar to a latte but with more foam and a sprinkle of cocoa powder.
  • Cold Brew: Coffee brewed with cold water for an extended period, resulting in a smooth flavor.
  • Flavored Coffee: A variety of coffee blends infused with flavors such as vanilla, caramel, or hazelnut.

2. How should I store my coffee beans?

To maintain the freshness and flavor of your coffee beans, it's essential to store them properly. Keep the beans in an airtight container to protect them from moisture and air, which can cause oxidation and loss of flavor. Store the container in a cool, dark place, away from heat and sunlight, which can degrade the beans' quality over time. Avoid storing coffee in the fridge or freezer, as the fluctuating temperatures and moisture can negatively affect the beans. Instead, aim to use the beans within a few weeks of opening for the best taste.

3. What grind size should I use for different brewing methods?

The grind size of your coffee plays a significant role in determining the flavor and extraction of your brew. Here’s a quick guide:

  • Coarse grind: Ideal for French press and cold brew, as the larger grounds allow for slow extraction over a longer period.
  • Medium grind: Best for drip coffee makers, pour-over methods, and Aeropress (with a shorter brew time). This grind size strikes a balance between quick extraction and a flavorful brew.
  • Fine grind: Suitable for espresso machines, as the smaller particles allow for quick extraction under pressure. This grind creates a strong, concentrated shot of coffee.
  • Extra fine grind: Typically used for Turkish coffee, where the grounds are almost powder-like to produce a thick, bold brew.

4. How long do coffee beans stay fresh?

Coffee beans are at their freshest within 2-4 weeks of roasting. After this period, they may begin to lose their aromatic oils and flavors. Once you open a bag of coffee beans, it’s best to consume them within a month for the richest taste. To maximize freshness, store the beans in an airtight container away from light, air, and moisture. Ground coffee loses freshness even faster, so it's recommended to grind your beans just before brewing.

6. What are the health benefits of coffee?

Moderate coffee consumption can offer several health benefits, including:

  • Improved Mental Alertness: Caffeine can enhance focus and concentration.
  • Rich in Antioxidants: Coffee contains antioxidants, which can help combat free radicals in the body.
  • Reduced Risk of Certain Diseases: Studies suggest that coffee drinkers may have a lower risk of certain conditions, such as Parkinson’s disease, type 2 diabetes, and some types of cancer.
